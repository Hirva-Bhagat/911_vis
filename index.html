"911 Calls in Pennsylvania"

  • The efficient management and provision of 911 calls have been consistently challenging.

  • Several stakeholder groups have been trying to collect, use, and analyze the relevant information in the last years to resolve the issues such as inadequate resources, lag time for a proper response, and other consequences.

  • A systemized plan for a more effective management plan will benefit residents and visitors.

  • Such a proper plan certainly requires a comprehensive understanding of the statistics, motivates a compelling information visualization, which our group has been trying to provide.

Annual 911 calls in Pennsylvania

  • Most 911 calls are related to emergency.
  • Most 911 calls happen during daytime.
  • It seems that there is a need for emergency-related infrastructures in Pennsylvania to respond citizens needs in a timely manner.

Subcategories of 911 calls in Pennsylvania

  • Some of the subcategories of three major reasons of calls (emergency, traffic and fire) are vehicle acciedents, fire alarms, respiratory emergencies, cardiac emergencies, head inguries, etc.
  • Traffic issues, particularly car accidents and disabled vehicles, are the most common reason of the calls annually.
  • The least common reasons of 911 calls in this state are bomb device-related calls, plane crash, train crash and active shootings.
  • Reasons of calls give a better picture of what managers should be prepared for. Emergency and accident related calls such as vehicle accidents, respiratory and caridac diseases are among the top reasons of calls and need extra preparedness
